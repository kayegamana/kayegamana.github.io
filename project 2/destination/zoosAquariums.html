Explore Tokyo: Zoos & Aquariums

Your Guide to Tokyo's Iconic Sites

Zoos & Aquariums

Ueno Zoological Gardens

Location: Ueno Park, Tokyo

Japan's oldest zoo, home to over 3,000 animals, including giant pandas and a wide variety of species.

Experience: Visitors can explore naturalistic exhibits, attend feeding sessions, and enjoy the surrounding park's cultural attractions.

Sunshine Aquarium

Location: Ikebukuro, Tokyo

An urban aquarium showcasing marine life, including a rooftop area featuring sea lions and interactive exhibits.

Experience: Ideal for families, it offers engaging presentations and unique underwater views, making it a delightful visit for ocean enthusiasts.

Tama Zoological Park

Location: Hino, Tokyo

A large, naturally landscaped zoo focusing on conservation, featuring a variety of animals in expansive habitats.

Experience: Offers interactive experiences and animal encounters, with educational programs that highlight wildlife preservation efforts.

Sumida Aquarium

Location: Tokyo Skytree Town, Sumida, Tokyo

Known for its innovative exhibits, including a large tank featuring diverse marine life and beautiful jellyfish displays.

Experience: Visitors can immerse themselves in a unique aquatic experience and enjoy interactive educational displays.

Tokyo Sea Life Park

Location: Edogawa, Tokyo

Highlights marine biodiversity, showcasing fish and sea life from various regions, with a large central tank.

Experience: Offers an informative and engaging visit with interactive exhibits and educational programs for all ages.
