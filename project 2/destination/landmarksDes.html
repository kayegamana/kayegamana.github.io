Explore Tokyo: Landmarks

Your Guide to Tokyo's Iconic Sites

Landmarks

Tokyo Skytree

Location: Sumida, Tokyo

Standing at 634 meters, it's the tallest structure in Japan, featuring two observation decks and a shopping complex at its base.

Experience: Visitors can enjoy breathtaking views of the Tokyo skyline, dine in restaurants, and shop for unique souvenirs, especially stunning during nighttime illuminations.

Sensoji Temple

Location: Asakusa, Tokyo

Tokyo's oldest temple, founded in 628 AD, with the iconic Kaminarimon Gate and a bustling shopping street (Nakamise-dori).

Experience: Immerse yourself in Japanese culture, witness traditional ceremonies, and sample local snacks as you stroll through the temple grounds.

Tokyo Tower

Location: Minato, Tokyo

A 333-meter tall communications tower, inspired by the Eiffel Tower, featuring observation decks with glass floors.

Experience: Enjoy panoramic views, visit exhibitions on its history, and dine in restaurants with stunning views of the city.

Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building

Location: Shinjuku, Tokyo

A striking example of postmodern architecture with two observation decks offering free access.

Experience: Enjoy unobstructed views of Tokyo and on clear days, even Mount Fuji, making it a perfect photo opportunity.

Azuma-bashi Bridge

Location: Asakusa, crossing the Sumida River

A historically significant bridge offering beautiful views of the Tokyo Skytree and the surrounding area.

Experience: A great spot for photography, especially during cherry blossom season, and serves as a link to various riverside attractions.
