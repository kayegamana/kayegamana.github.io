Your Guide to Tokyo's Iconic Sites

Parks & Gardens

Shinjuku Gyoen National Gardens

Location: Shinjuku, Tokyo

A spacious park combining traditional Japanese, French, and English garden designs, featuring cherry blossoms in spring.

Experience: A serene retreat from the city, perfect for picnics, leisurely strolls, and enjoying the beauty of seasonal flowers.

Hama-rikyu Gardens

Location: Chuo, Tokyo

Historical gardens with tidal ponds, traditional tea houses, and a blend of Edo-period landscapes.

Experience: Visitors can explore beautiful walking paths and enjoy tea ceremonies while taking in views of the Tokyo skyline.

Yoyogi Park

Location: Shibuya, Tokyo

A large urban park popular for outdoor activities, picnics, and festivals, often bustling with energy.

Experience: Ideal for relaxation, jogging, or enjoying vibrant cultural events, with easy access to nearby Meiji Shrine.

Inokashira Park

Location: Mitaka, Tokyo

A scenic park featuring a pond, rental boats, a small zoo, and lush greenery.

Experience: Perfect for families, visitors can enjoy paddle boating, explore the Ghibli Museum, and experience local wildlife.

Rikugien Gardens

Location: Bunkyo, Tokyo

A traditional Japanese landscape garden known for its stunning seasonal views, particularly during autumn foliage.

Experience: A peaceful retreat featuring picturesque walking paths, teahouses, and opportunities for photography and meditation.
