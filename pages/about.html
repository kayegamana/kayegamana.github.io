ACT I

Origin.

The curiosity arrives before the discipline does.

2018 – 2022 · Calamba City Science Integrated School

I grew up in Calamba, in a household where you finished what you started and you finished it cleanly. Curiosity wasn't a personality trait there — it was the cost of admission. STEM at CCSIS was where I first met the kind of problems where the answer isn't googleable, just smaller problems all the way down.

I graduated with Highest Honors — 18th out of 376 — and the rank meant less to me than the habit it built. The habit was: read it twice, draw it once, then write it. Same habit I still use when a schema gives me trouble.

Research papers were where I figured out that I liked the part nobody else liked. Not the conclusion — the part where you sit with a half-broken hypothesis for two weeks and wait for it to either survive or fall over.

“

Curiosity is the only thing in this house you don't have to ask permission for.

— something my parents basically said with their actions, on repeat

ACT II

Discipline.

Curiosity, but trained — and given a stack to work with.

2022 – 2026 · Polytechnic University of the Philippines, BSIT
RECORD 4 / 4 Years on the President's List · PUP Sta. Mesa
SCHOLARSHIP RA 7687 Department of Science and Technology - SEI · National science scholarship
STACK GAINED PHP · MySQL · JavaScript · Bootstrap · Figma · UML · Git · OOP · SDLC · DBA basics · QA fundamentals

PUP is where the curiosity got walls. I learned that a system isn't done when it works once — it's done when it survives a second person opening the codebase. Capstone projects, group leads, schemas drawn three times before they got committed.

I ran the spine of more group projects than I can count, partly because I liked making things readable for the next teammate, and partly because the alternative was watching the build fall over at 3 a.m. before defense. I also got picky about details — about contrasts, vertical rhythm, why a button should never argue with its label.

Got the Department of Science and Technology - SEI RA 7687 scholarship along the way, which meant the science stayed in front of the engineering. I still think of it as a quiet contract: do work that's worth the country having paid for it.

ACT III

Practice.

Where the code meets a calendar, a user, and a deadline.

Feb – May 2026 · ArkTech Philippines · Full-Stack Intern

I just wrapped four months at ArkTech Philippines as a full-stack developer intern. Three internal systems shipped — all of them now sitting inside the company's day-to-day operations. Real users hit my forms; real bugs hit my inbox.

That's the part school doesn't quite teach you — that production is a different animal than presentation. It doesn't care about the demo flow. It cares about that one user who pastes an emoji into a phone-number field on a Tuesday afternoon, and the schema you had better have hardened against it. I am — happily — the kind of engineer who finds that funny.

Graduating in a few weeks. Open to full-time roles, freelance, and any project where craft actually matters. I read every message. I reply to most within a day.
